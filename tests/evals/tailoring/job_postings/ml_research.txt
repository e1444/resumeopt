About the role

We are hiring a Machine Learning Research Engineer to develop generative modeling methods for structured prediction problems. You will design new model architectures, run rigorous benchmark comparisons, and communicate calibrated, reproducible results.

What you'll do
- Design and implement generative models (e.g. normalizing flows, diffusion, or related likelihood-based approaches) for classification and structured-prediction tasks.
- Formulate and solve constrained optimization problems that trade off predictive accuracy against other objectives such as calibration or fairness.
- Build and maintain a configurable experimentation framework (e.g. Hydra-style configs) to run and track hyperparameter sweeps.
- Use experiment tracking tools (e.g. Weights & Biases) to log, compare, and reproduce results across runs.
- Evaluate models against standard benchmarks (e.g. MNIST, CIFAR) using calibration metrics (ECE) and generative-quality metrics (bits/dim, FID) in addition to accuracy.
- Write up findings clearly, including ablations and comparisons against baselines.

What we're looking for
- Strong background in generative modeling and probabilistic machine learning.
- Experience formulating and solving constrained or multi-objective optimization problems.
- Hands-on experience with modern experiment configuration/tracking tooling (Hydra, W&B, or similar).
- Comfort reporting calibration and generative-quality metrics, not just accuracy.
- Strong Python skills.

Nice to have
- Publications or open-source contributions in generative modeling or calibration.
- Experience with distributed/multi-GPU training.
